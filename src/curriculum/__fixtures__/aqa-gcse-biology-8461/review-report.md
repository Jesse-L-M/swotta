## Misconceptions, Task Rules, and Source Mappings
Misconception rules (12 rules)
- 4.1.3.2 Osmosis | severity 3 | Treats osmosis as antibiotic molecules entering bacteria rather than water moving across a partially permeable membrane.
  triggers: antibiotic enters by osmosis; osmosis means chemicals move into bacteria; osmosis moves the antibiotic into the cell
  correction: Osmosis is the net movement of water through a partially permeable membrane from higher water potential to lower water potential.
- 4.2.1 Principles of organisation | severity 2 | Treats a tissue as any group of cells and omits the idea that the cells are similar in structure or function.
  triggers: tissue is a group of cells; tissues are many cells together; any cells working together make a tissue
  correction: A tissue is a group of similar cells organised to carry out a particular shared function.
- 4.2.2.2 The heart and blood vessels | severity 3 | Confuses the role of the coronary arteries with vessels that carry blood to the lungs or around the rest of the body.
  triggers: coronary arteries take blood to the lungs; coronary arteries move blood around the body; blocked coronary artery stops blood reaching the lungs
  correction: Coronary arteries supply oxygenated blood to the heart muscle itself, so blockage reduces aerobic respiration in cardiac muscle.
- 4.3.1.3 Bacterial diseases | severity 2 | Knows that toxins matter in salmonella infection but does not state that the bacteria release or secrete them.
  triggers: salmonella has toxins; salmonella contains toxin; toxin inside the bacteria causes diarrhoea
  correction: Salmonella bacteria produce and release toxins, and those toxins damage the body and cause symptoms such as diarrhoea and vomiting.
- 4.3.1.7 Vaccination | severity 3 | Mixes up antigens, antibodies, antitoxins and memory cells when explaining vaccination.
  triggers: memory cells are antibodies; antibodies make white blood cells; vaccine gives antitoxins directly
  correction: Vaccination introduces antigens, stimulates antibody production, and leaves memory cells that speed up the secondary response on re-exposure.
- 4.4.2.1 Aerobic and anaerobic respiration | severity 2 | Thinks plants respire only at night rather than continuously.
  triggers: plants respire at night only; plants stop respiration in daylight; respiration happens only when photosynthesis stops
  correction: Plants respire all the time; photosynthesis happens only in light, but respiration continues day and night.
- 4.5.2.3 The eye (biology only) | severity 2 | Describes the lens as larger or more concave when focusing on a near object.
  triggers: lens gets larger for near focus; lens becomes concave to see near objects; lens stretches flatter for nearby objects
  correction: To focus on a near object the lens becomes thicker, rounder and more convex.
- 4.5.3.3 Maintaining water and nitrogen balance in the body (biology only) | severity 3 | Has weak or fragmented understanding of kidney function and the role of the kidney in homeostasis.
  triggers: kidneys remove all water; kidneys only store urine; kidneys make urea
  correction: The kidneys filter the blood, selectively reabsorb useful substances and water, and regulate water and ion balance while urea is excreted in urine.
- 4.6.1.1 Sexual and asexual reproduction | severity 2 | Treats tissue culture as simply using tissue instead of growing cells to produce clones.
  triggers: tissue culture means using plant tissue; tissue culture is cutting up tissue only; tissue culture means growing tissue without cells
  correction: Tissue culture grows plant cells or small tissue samples by mitosis to produce many genetically identical clones.
- 4.6.1.2 Meiosis | severity 3 | Confuses meiosis with mitosis or cannot use meiosis to explain genetic variation.
  triggers: meiosis makes identical cells; meiosis has one division only; meiosis is for growth and repair
  correction: Meiosis halves the chromosome number, produces genetically different gametes, and underpins variation in sexual reproduction.
- 4.6.3.7 Resistant bacteria | severity 3 | Says bacteria become immune to antibiotics instead of resistant to them.
  triggers: bacteria become immune to antibiotics; animal becomes immune to antibiotics; antibiotic creates immunity in bacteria
  correction: Random mutations can make some bacteria resistant to an antibiotic, and selection increases the frequency of resistant strains.
- 4.7.1.1 Communities | severity 2 | Uses ecosystem when the question is asking for the community of organisms living together in a habitat.
  triggers: all organisms in a habitat make an ecosystem only; community means one population; habitat equals community
  correction: A community is all the populations of different species living together in a habitat, while an ecosystem also includes the abiotic environment.

Task rules (9 rules)
- 4.5.3.3 Maintaining water and nitrogen balance in the body (biology only) | high reentry | Re-enter kidney questions through the blood-filter-reabsorb-excrete sequence
  guidance: If kidney questions are going badly, route the learner back through the ordered sequence blood filtration -> selective reabsorption -> urine formation before asking for detail.
  conditions: kidney topic; repeated failure; homeostasis unit
- 4.6.1.2 Meiosis | high retrieval_drill | Contrast meiosis with mitosis before genetics problems
  guidance: Use short retrieval prompts that force chromosome number, number of divisions, cell type produced and genetic similarity for both meiosis and mitosis.
  conditions: reproduction topic; genetics problem; low-confidence response history
- 4.6.3.7 Resistant bacteria | high retrieval_drill | Separate resistance from immunity
  guidance: Use short retrieval drills that contrast resistant bacteria, immune individuals, and the selective pressure produced by antibiotic use.
  conditions: antibiotic-resistance question; low-confidence response history
- 4.1.3.2 Osmosis | high worked_example | Model osmosis with water-potential language
  guidance: Use worked examples that force learners to name water as the moving substance, identify the partially permeable membrane, and state the direction of net movement.
  conditions: osmosis topic; membrane-transport explanation; prior misconception triggered
- 4.2.2.2 The heart and blood vessels | high worked_example | Build coronary-artery explanations as a causal chain
  guidance: Rehearse the chain blocked coronary artery -> less oxygen to heart muscle -> less aerobic respiration -> less energy released -> reduced pumping efficiency / fatigue / heart attack risk.
  conditions: command word explain; open-response question; circulation topic
- 4.3.1.7 Vaccination | high worked_example | Sequence vaccination responses explicitly
  guidance: Make students state antigen, antibody, memory cell, and faster secondary response in order before attempting prose answers about vaccination.
  conditions: vaccination topic; immune-response explanation; command word explain
- 4.4.2.1 Aerobic and anaerobic respiration | medium explanation | State that plants respire continuously before discussing day-night differences
  guidance: Require the learner to say 'plants respire all the time' before adding what changes between day and night in photosynthesis and gas exchange.
  conditions: respiration topic; plant processes question
- 4.6.2.3 Selective breeding | medium mistake_review | Lock down control variables and sample-size reasoning in breeding investigations
  guidance: Review answers that use vague environment wording and replace them with named control variables plus a clear reason why larger sample sizes improve representativeness and reduce anomaly effects.
  conditions: investigation planning; selective-breeding context; variable-control question
- 4.5.2.3 The eye (biology only) | medium worked_example | Rehearse accommodation as muscle-lens-pupil changes
  guidance: Model near and bright-light responses as linked changes in ciliary muscle, lens shape, iris muscles and pupil size, using precise anatomical vocabulary.
  conditions: eye topic; structure-and-function explanation; prior lens-shape misconception
