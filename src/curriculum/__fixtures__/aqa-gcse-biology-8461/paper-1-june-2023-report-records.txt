[misconception]
locator: Question 01.1
topicRef: 4.2.1
description: Treats a tissue as any group of cells and omits the idea that the cells are similar in structure or function.
triggerPatterns: tissue is a group of cells || tissues are many cells together || any cells working together make a tissue
correctionGuidance: A tissue is a group of similar cells organised to carry out a particular shared function.
severity: 2

[misconception]
locator: Question 02.8
topicRef: 4.2.2.2
description: Confuses the role of the coronary arteries with vessels that carry blood to the lungs or around the rest of the body.
triggerPatterns: coronary arteries take blood to the lungs || coronary arteries move blood around the body || blocked coronary artery stops blood reaching the lungs
correctionGuidance: Coronary arteries supply oxygenated blood to the heart muscle itself, so blockage reduces aerobic respiration in cardiac muscle.
severity: 3

[misconception]
locator: Question 03.1
topicRef: 4.3.1.3
description: Knows that toxins matter in salmonella infection but does not state that the bacteria release or secrete them.
triggerPatterns: salmonella has toxins || salmonella contains toxin || toxin inside the bacteria causes diarrhoea
correctionGuidance: Salmonella bacteria produce and release toxins, and those toxins damage the body and cause symptoms such as diarrhoea and vomiting.
severity: 2

[misconception]
locator: Question 03.2
topicRef: 4.3.1.7
description: Mixes up antigens, antibodies, antitoxins and memory cells when explaining vaccination.
triggerPatterns: memory cells are antibodies || antibodies make white blood cells || vaccine gives antitoxins directly
correctionGuidance: Vaccination introduces antigens, stimulates antibody production, and leaves memory cells that speed up the secondary response on re-exposure.
severity: 3

[misconception]
locator: Question 03.8
topicRef: 4.6.3.7
description: Says bacteria become immune to antibiotics instead of resistant to them.
triggerPatterns: bacteria become immune to antibiotics || animal becomes immune to antibiotics || antibiotic creates immunity in bacteria
correctionGuidance: Random mutations can make some bacteria resistant to an antibiotic, and selection increases the frequency of resistant strains.
severity: 3

[misconception]
locator: Question 03.9
topicRef: 4.1.3.2
description: Treats osmosis as antibiotic molecules entering bacteria rather than water moving across a partially permeable membrane.
triggerPatterns: antibiotic enters by osmosis || osmosis means chemicals move into bacteria || osmosis moves the antibiotic into the cell
correctionGuidance: Osmosis is the net movement of water through a partially permeable membrane from higher water potential to lower water potential.
severity: 3
