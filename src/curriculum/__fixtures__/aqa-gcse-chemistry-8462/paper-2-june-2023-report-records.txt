locator: Question 03.2 and 03.3

description: Uses the abbreviation FES or describes flame emission spectroscopy as more precise rather than naming it fully and explaining its greater accuracy.
triggerPatterns: use fes || flame emission is more precise || instrumental method gives more ions at once so that is the main advantage

[misconception]
locator: Question 08.8
topicRef: 4.7.3.4
description: Answers DNA-building-unit questions with bases or the names of bases instead of nucleotides.
triggerPatterns: dna is made of bases || adenine thymine cytosine guanine are the repeating units || dna is made from amino acids
correctionGuidance: DNA is a polymer of nucleotides. Each nucleotide includes a sugar, a phosphate group, and one base, so bases alone are not the repeating unit.
severity: 2

[misconception]
locator: Question 09.5 and 09.6
topicRef: 4.6.2.3
description: Claims that a catalyst shifts equilibrium to the products or ignores that equal total moles on both sides mean pressure does not change the equilibrium position.
triggerPatterns: catalyst shifts equilibrium to the right || pressure always moves equilibrium to the side with fewer moles even when the totals are equal || faster rate means higher equilibrium yield
correctionGuidance: A catalyst speeds up both forward and reverse reactions equally and does not change equilibrium position. Pressure only changes equilibrium when the total gaseous moles differ between sides.
severity: 3
